Parts of Speech MCQs

Key Rules:

  • Noun: Naming word (Person, Place, Thing). Example: Lahore is a big city.
  • Verb: Action word. Example: She runs fast.
  • Adjective: Describes a noun. Example: Beautiful flower.

Active & Passive Voice

Basic Formula:

  • Active: Subject + Verb + Object
  • Passive: Object + Helping Verb + V3 + by + Subject
  • Example: He writes a letter. → A letter is written by him.

Prepositions

Important Rules:

  • At: Specific time/place (at 5 PM, at home)
  • In: Months/Years/Large places (in July, in Pakistan)
  • On: Days/Surfaces (on Monday, on the table)

Pair of Words

Common Pairs:

  • Affect (v) vs Effect (n): The news affected him. The effect was bad.
  • Accept (v) vs Except (prep): I accept your offer. Everyone came except Ali.

Comprehension Paragraph

Tips:

  • Read the passage twice before answering.
  • Answers should be in your own words.
  • Keep answers short and to the point.

Use of It & There

Rules:

  • It is: Used for specific things/time/weather. It is raining. It is a book.
  • There is/are: Used for existence. There is a book on the table. There are many students.

Am, Is, Are, Was, Were

Usage:

  • Am: Only with 'I'. I am happy.
  • Is: Singular (He/She/It). He is tall.
  • Are: Plural (We/You/They). They are playing.
  • Was/Were: Past tense forms of Is/Are.

Has, Have, Had

Rules:

  • Has: Singular subjects (He/She/It). He has a car.
  • Have: Plural subjects & I/You. I have a pen. They have books.
  • Had: Past tense for all subjects. She had gone yesterday.
